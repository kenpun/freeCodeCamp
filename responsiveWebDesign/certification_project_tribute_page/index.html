Michael Pollan

Bestselling Author & Journalist

Here is a short bio of Michael Pollan's work:

Current Affiliation

  • 1995 - present - Contributing writer, New York Times Magazine
  • 2017 - present - Professor of the Practice of non-Fiction and Lewis K. Chan Arts Lecturer, Harvard University
  • 2021 - present - Professor of the Graduate School, University of California, Berkeley

Publications: Books

  • 2001 - The Botany of Desire: A Plant's-Eye View of the World (Random House)
  • 2006 - The Omnivore's Dilemma: A Natural History of Four Meals (The Penguin Press)
  • 2008 - In Defense of Food: An Eater's Manifesto (The Penguin Press)
  • 2010 - Food Rules: An Eater's Manual (The Penguin Press)
  • 2013 - Cooked: A Natural History of Transformation (The Penguin Press)
  • 2018 - How to Change Your Mind: What the New Science of PsychedelicsTeaches Us About Consciousness, Dying, Addiction, Depression, and Transcendence (The Penguin Press)

Film and Television Projects

  • 2009 - Special Consultant and co-narrator, Food Inc.
  • 2009 - Host and executive producer of The Botany of Desire
  • 2015 - Host and narrator of In Defense of Food
  • 2016 - Host and narrator of Cooked: A Natural History of Four Meals
  • 2022 - Host and narrator of How To Change Your Mind
